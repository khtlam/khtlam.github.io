About Me

Terry is graduated from the Department of Mathematics in HKUST. During his undergraduate studies, he not only studied advanced Mathematics and Computer Science but has also developed STEM-related teaching materials for high school students to understand functions in Teaching Science using Innovative Tools course. His capstone project is about improvement and modification on the Hill Cipher Cryptosystem by Image Extension, which is an application in Linear Algebra.

Terry loves to solve Mathematics problems. Therefore, he also studied Mathematics Problem Solving and Geometric Constructions in HKUST which the smart techniques in the course contents are extremely useful for the Mathematics Olympiad Competition. He was one of the volunteers and team guide of the International Mathematics Olympiad (IMO), which is the largest Mathematics competition in the world for secondary school students. He thinks this is one of the best experiences he has ever had in his life.

In his leisure time, he enjoys doing productive work. He will treat writing a program or webpage as an interest. On top of studying, he is also a strong passionate teacher and has experience in teaching students from different backgrounds and at different levels. He has been serving as a teaching assistant of Physics and Mathematics courses for elites and gifted secondary school students in the dual program and Summer Institute in his home university. He loves devoting his time to write new teaching notes for their students on weekends. He also likes to discuss mathematics problems with students as well.

Terry also joins a lot of services in his study life. He was the chairperson of Mathematics Society in his secondary school. At the same time, he was also the general secretary of the Students' Association. In his university life, he is the general secretary of the Mathematics Students' Society. He always shares his university life in the information day and interview day.
